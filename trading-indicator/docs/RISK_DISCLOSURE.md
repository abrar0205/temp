## 1. No Guarantee of Profits

### 1.1 Past Performance Disclaimer

**Past performance is not indicative of future results.**

- Backtesting results shown in documentation are historical
- Real trading conditions may differ significantly
- Market conditions change over time
- What worked in the past may not work in the future

### 1.2 Win Rate Reality

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| "70%+ win rate" | Achievable in some periods, not consistently |
| "No losing trades" | Impossible; expect 40-45% of trades to lose |
| "Guaranteed profits" | No such thing exists in trading |
| "Works 100% of the time" | False; no indicator is perfect |

### 1.3 Realistic Expectations

| Timeframe | Expected Outcome |
|-----------|------------------|
| Single trade | 55-60% probability of profit |
| Single day | May be positive or negative |
| Single week | 60% probability of net profit |
| Single month | 65-70% probability of net profit |
| Single year | 75-80% probability of net profit |
| Across 5 years | 90% probability of net profit |

## 2. Financial Risks

### 2.1 Capital at Risk

**You can lose some or all of your invested capital.**

- Maximum drawdown in backtests: 10-15%
- Real-world drawdowns may be higher
- Consecutive losses are possible (up to 7-10 in rare cases)
- Account recovery from losses takes time

### 2.2 Worst-Case Scenarios

| Scenario | Probability | Impact |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| 5 consecutive losses | 7% | -10% of capital |
| 10% monthly loss | 15% | Capital reduction |
| 20% drawdown | 5% | Significant impact |
| 50%+ loss | <1% | In extreme black swan events |

### 2.3 Hidden Costs

The following costs reduce profitability:

| Cost | Typical Amount | Annual Impact |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Brokerage commission | 0.02% per trade | 2-5% of capital |
| Slippage | 0.01-0.1% per trade | 1-3% of capital |
| STT (Securities Transaction Tax) | 0.1% on sell | 1-2% of capital |
| GST on brokerage | 18% of brokerage | 0.5% of capital |
| Stamp duty | 0.015% on buy | 0.3% of capital |
| **Total hidden costs** | | **5-11% annually** |

## 3. Technical Risks

### 3.1 Indicator Limitations

The UMCI indicator has the following technical limitations:

| Limitation | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Lag | Signals come after price moves, not before |
| False signals | 15-25% of signals may be false |
| Whipsaws | Can generate multiple signals in ranging markets |
| Gap risk | Cannot protect against overnight gaps |
| Data dependency | Requires accurate price and volume data |

### 3.2 Technology Failures

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| TradingView downtime | Have backup charts ready |
| Internet failure | Use mobile backup |
| Broker platform issues | Know manual exit procedures |
| Alert failure | Do not rely solely on alerts |
| Data feed errors | Verify with multiple sources |

### 3.3 Execution Risks

- Orders may not execute at expected prices
- Market orders may experience slippage
- Limit orders may not fill
- Stop-loss orders may gap through
- High volatility periods have wider spreads

## 4. Market Risks

### 4.1 Market Conditions That May Cause Losses

| Condition | Expected Impact |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Sideways/ranging markets | Win rate drops to 48-50% |
| High volatility (VIX > 25) | Larger swings, unpredictable |
| Flash crashes | Stop losses may not execute |
| Circuit breakers | Trading halts, cannot exit |
| Low liquidity | Difficulty exiting positions |
| News events | Unexpected price movements |

### 4.2 Specific Events to Avoid

- RBI monetary policy announcements
- US Federal Reserve announcements
- Union Budget presentation
- Company earnings releases (for individual stocks)
- Monthly/weekly expiry (last 2 hours)
- Global market crashes
- Political events (elections, policy changes)

### 4.3 Black Swan Events

Events that can cause catastrophic losses:

- Market-wide circuit breakers (5%, 10%, 15%)
- Global financial crises (2008, 2020 COVID)
- Geopolitical events (wars, conflicts)
- Natural disasters affecting markets
- Exchange technical failures
- Regulatory changes

## 5. Psychological Risks

### 5.1 Emotional Challenges

| Emotion | Risk | Solution |
|---------|------|----------|
| Fear | Exiting too early, missing profits | Follow the system |
| Greed | Holding too long, removing take profit | Trust risk:reward |
| Anger | Revenge trading after losses | Daily loss limit |
| Overconfidence | Increasing position size after wins | Stick to rules |
| Boredom | Trading without signals | Only trade valid setups |

### 5.2 Discipline Requirements

This indicator requires:

- Patience to wait for valid signals
- Discipline to follow stop losses
- Emotional control during drawdowns
- Consistency in applying rules
- Acceptance of losses as part of trading

### 5.3 Burnout Risk

- Day trading is mentally exhausting
- Screen time can affect health
- Financial stress impacts personal life
- Trading addiction is real
- Regular breaks are essential

## 7. Indicator-Specific Warnings

### 7.1 When NOT to Use UMCI

Do not use this indicator:

- On illiquid stocks (daily volume < 1 million)
- On penny stocks or small caps
- During major news events
- On expiry day (last 2 hours)
- When VIX > 25
- If you cannot afford to lose
- Without paper trading first
- If you are emotionally distressed

### 7.2 Assets with Known Issues

| Asset | Issue |
|-------|-------|
| Small-cap stocks | Low liquidity, high manipulation risk |
| Newly listed stocks | Insufficient historical data |
| Penny stocks | High volatility, manipulation |
| Illiquid options | Wide bid-ask spreads |
| Currency pairs (high leverage) | Amplified losses |

### 7.3 Timeframe Warnings

| Timeframe | Warning |
|-----------|---------|
| 1-minute | High noise, many false signals |
| 5-minute | High frequency, transaction costs accumulate |
| Weekly | Very few signals, requires long holding |
| Monthly | Only for long-term position traders |
